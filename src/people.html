Eden Brachot

Project Director, Beebe Lake Redevelopment

Eden is a senior in Design and Environmental Analysis pursuing a concentration in Interior Design. As a designer, she is intrigued by the thought that she can impact the way people feel upon entering a space. As a critical thinker, she believes this impact is achieved by weighing the grand concept of a project as heavily as every minor detail. Eden originally joined the NYC Tech Campus project on CUSD to help design a distance-learning classroom. She then joined the Beebe Lake Redevelopment project. After a total of four semesters on CUSD, she currently directs the Beebe Lake Redevelopment project. Eden's dream job is to oversee the interior design of cruise ships and believes that long meetings should always be accompanied by good food.

Zac Cesaro

Deputy Project Director, CornellTech and Re-Innovations

Zac Cesaro is a junior at Cornell University majoring in Chemical Engineering. Zac is the Deputy Project Director for NYCTech and Re-Innovations projects of the Cornell University Sustainable Design project team as well as the Team Lead for the Solar Energy project team.   Zac participated in undergraduate research at Cornell under Dr. Paul Steen, studying planar flow casting and X-ray Diffraction of amorphous (glassy) metals. Over summer 2014, Zac worked in Morgantown, WV for the U.S. Department of Energy's fossil fuel research lab studying nanoparticle catalysts for hydrocarbon reforming. Zac was also a member of the Varsity Men’s Swimming and Diving team at Cornell from 2012-2014.  Zac’s career aspirations include working in the energy industry on projects that emphasize sustainability and renewability.  He views widespread access to sustainable energy as one of the worlds largest problems that has tremendous room for innovation and improvement. Zac's primary focus is to help society with both immediate needs and long term sustainability.
